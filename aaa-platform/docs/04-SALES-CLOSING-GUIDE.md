# Sales Closing Guide: The 11-Step Strategy Session

This guide details the complete sales process for closing Tier 3 high-ticket clients ($2,500-$5,000).

---

## Core Philosophy

> **No pain, no sale.**

We are not salespeople. We are **diagnostic consultants**—doctors who uncover the cost of a problem before prescribing the solution.

The sale happens when the prospect realizes the cost of NOT solving their problem exceeds the cost of your solution.

---

## Pre-Call Preparation

### Before every strategy call:

1. **Research the prospect** (15 min)
   - LinkedIn profile and activity
   - Company website
   - Recent posts/content
   - Tech stack (check their site source, job posts)

2. **Prepare questions** based on research
   - What might their pain be based on their industry?
   - What have they tried before?
   - What's their likely budget?

3. **Set your environment**
   - Quiet space, good lighting
   - Camera on, professional background
   - Water nearby
   - Stripe ready to take payment

---

## The 11-Step Strategy Session Protocol

### Step 1: Open/Connect (2-3 min)

**Goal**: Put the prospect at ease. Build rapport without wasting time.

**Script**:

```text
"Hey [Name], great to meet you! Before we dive in, I saw you're based in 
[city]—how's the weather there today?"

[Brief small talk, max 2 minutes]

"Alright, let me respect your time and get us started..."
```

**Do**:
- Smile genuinely
- Find one personal connection
- Keep it brief

**Don't**:
- Talk about yourself
- Let small talk exceed 3 minutes
- Start pitching

---

### Step 2: Take the Lead (1-2 min)

**Goal**: Establish authority and set the agenda. You control the call.

**Script**:

```text
"Here's how I like to structure these calls. I'll ask you some questions 
about your business, what's working, what's not. Then if I think I can 
help, I'll share how—and if not, I'll point you in the right direction 
either way. Sound fair?"
```

**Why this works**:
- Sets expectation that this is a diagnosis, not a pitch
- Positions you as the expert
- Gets permission to ask deep questions

---

### Step 3: Explore Motive (2-3 min)

**Goal**: Understand why they're here NOW. Timing reveals urgency.

**Key Questions**:

```text
"What made you book this call today? Like, why now specifically?"

"What's been going on in your business that made this a priority?"

"Have you tried to solve this before? What happened?"
```

**Listen for**:
- Recent pain trigger (lost client, burnout, missed opportunity)
- Failed attempts (DIY, previous consultant)
- External pressure (investor, spouse, deadline)

**Note**: If there's no urgency, they won't buy today. Your job is to uncover or create urgency.

---

### Step 4: Understand Context (5-7 min)

**Goal**: Map their current state—the "Now".

**Key Questions**:

```text
"Walk me through your business. What do you sell, and who do you sell it to?"

"What does a typical week look like for you operationally?"

"How much revenue are you doing right now?"

"What's your current tech stack? What tools are you using day-to-day?"
```

**What to capture**:
- Business model
- Revenue range
- Team size (if any)
- Tech tools in use
- Time spent on operations

**Pro tip**: Take notes. Repeat back what you hear. "So if I'm hearing you right..."

---

### Step 5: Examine Pain (10-15 min)

**Goal**: Uncover the HURT. This is the most important step.

**The Pain Deep-Dive Questions**:

```text
"What's the #1 thing that's preventing you from growing right now?"

"How long have you been dealing with this?"

"What does this actually cost you—in time, money, or stress?"

"Why do you think this problem exists? What's causing it?"

"What happens if this doesn't get fixed in the next 6 months?"
```

**Quantify the pain**:

```text
"You mentioned you spend 10 hours a week on [task]. At your hourly rate, 
that's roughly $X per week, or $Y per year. Does that sound right?"
```

**Emotional depth**:

```text
"How does this affect you personally? Like, when you're done for the day, 
are you actually done—or is this stuff still on your mind?"
```

**Rules**:
- Let them talk 80%, you talk 20%
- Use silence—count to 5 before responding
- Never minimize their pain

---

### Step 6: Understand Dreams (3-5 min)

**Goal**: Define the ideal "Future" 12 months out.

**Key Questions**:

```text
"Let's paint a picture. It's 12 months from now, and this automation 
project has been wildly successful. What does your business look like?"

"What would change in your day-to-day life?"

"What revenue would you be at?"

"How many hours a week would you actually be working?"
```

**What to capture**:
- Financial goals
- Lifestyle goals
- Freedom/time goals
- Emotional state they want

---

### Step 7: Dig Below the Surface (5-7 min)

**Goal**: Uncover the REAL motivation—the emotional driver.

**Key Questions**:

```text
"Why is hitting $[revenue goal] actually important to you?"

"What does that represent for you?"

"If you achieved that, what would that mean for your family?"

"What would it feel like to have 10 extra hours a week?"
```

**Keep digging**:
- First answer = surface answer
- Second answer = getting warmer
- Third answer = the truth

**Example flow**:

```text
You: "Why is $30K/month important?"
Them: "Because I want financial security."
You: "What does financial security mean to you?"
Them: "Not worrying about bills."
You: "And if you weren't worried about bills, what would that allow you to do?"
Them: "Actually be present with my kids instead of stressed about work."
```

**Now you have emotional fuel for the close.**

---

### Step 8: Identify the Gap (3-5 min)

**Goal**: Make the gap between "Now" and "Future" painfully clear.

**Script**:

```text
"So let me play this back. Right now, you're at $[X] revenue, spending 
[Y] hours a week on manual work, and feeling [emotion]. 

Your goal is to hit $[Z] revenue, work [fewer] hours, and feel [desired emotion].

That gap—from where you are to where you want to be—that's what we need to bridge.

Does that feel accurate?"
```

**Amplify the gap**:

```text
"What's it costing you to stay in this gap? Every month that passes, 
you're losing $[X] in potential revenue and [Y] hours you'll never get back."
```

---

### Step 9: Professional Advice (1-2 min)

**Goal**: Transition from diagnosis to prescription. Ask permission.

**Script**:

```text
"Based on everything you've shared, I have some ideas on how we could 
solve this. Would you like to hear how we might help?"
```

**Wait for a YES.** This is critical. They must invite the solution.

If they say no or hesitate:

```text
"Totally understand. Is there something else you were hoping to 
talk about today?"
```

---

### Step 10: State the Offer (5-7 min)

**Goal**: Present your solution clearly and concisely.

**Framework: The Branded Method**

```text
"What we do is called the Apex Automation Architecture. Here's how it works:

1. DIAGNOSE: We do a deep-dive into your operations—exactly like we did today.

2. DESIGN: Our AI generates a custom automation blueprint tailored to your 
   specific business, tech stack, and goals.

3. DEPLOY: Our team builds and implements the entire system for you. 
   No DIY, no figuring it out yourself.

4. SUPPORT: 90 days of support to refine and optimize.

The result? You go from spending [X hours] on [pain] to having it run 
automatically—giving you back [Y hours] per week to focus on growth."
```

**Handle the "What does that include?"**:

```text
- Full automation architecture design
- Implementation by our team (not you)
- Integration with your existing tools
- Training session for you and your team
- 90 days of priority support
```

---

### Step 11: The Close (5-10 min)

**Goal**: State the price, then STOP TALKING.

**Script**:

```text
"The investment for the full Apex Implementation is $[2,500/3,500/5,000].

Given what you told me—that this problem is costing you $[X]/month and 
[Y] hours per week—this pays for itself in [timeframe].

Does that feel like a fair investment to solve this problem permanently?"
```

**THE MOST IMPORTANT RULE: SHUT UP.**

After you state the price, DO NOT SPEAK. The first person to talk loses.

Count to 30 in your head if you need to. Let them process.

## Handling Objections

### "I need to think about it"

```text
"Totally fair. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the 
investment, the timing, or something about the solution itself?"
```

Then address that specific concern.

### "It's too expensive"

```text
"I understand. Let me ask—if the investment wasn't a factor, would this 
be the solution you'd want?"

If yes: "What would make the investment feel worth it?"

If no: "What's missing?"
```

### "I need to talk to my partner/spouse"

```text
"Of course. What do you think they'll say? Are they aware of the 
problem we discussed today? What questions might they have?"
```

Then offer to include them on a follow-up call.

### "Can I get a discount?"

```text
"I appreciate you asking. The pricing reflects the value and the level 
of service we provide. That said, if budget is a concern, we do have 
a payment plan option—would that help?"
```

## After the Call

### If They Close

1. Send Stripe payment link immediately
2. Send welcome email with next steps
3. Schedule kickoff call within 48 hours
4. Add to client management system

### If They Don't Close

1. Send follow-up email within 24 hours:

```text
Subject: Following up on our chat

Hey [Name],

Great speaking with you today. I know you mentioned wanting to think 
about it—totally understand.

Just wanted to leave you with this: every week you spend on [pain], 
you're losing [quantified cost]. The automation we discussed would 
eliminate that permanently.

If you have questions, just reply to this email. If you're ready to 
move forward, here's the link: [Stripe link]

[Your name]
```

2. Add to follow-up sequence (Day 3, Day 7, Day 14)
3. Note objection in CRM for future reference

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Talking too much** - The prospect should talk 70-80%
2. **Pitching too early** - No solution until Step 10
3. **Accepting vague answers** - Dig deeper
4. **Avoiding the price** - State it confidently
5. **Breaking silence** - Let them process
6. **Not quantifying pain** - Always tie to dollars or hours
7. **Chasing unqualified leads** - No pain = no sale

## Practice Makes Profit

Role-play these calls with a partner before doing them live. Record your calls (with permission) and review them weekly.

The goal: Make this process feel natural, not scripted.

When you truly believe in the transformation you provide, closing becomes serving.
